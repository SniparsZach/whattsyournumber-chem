Question 3 - Pentaerythritol Tetranitrate (PETN)

How Many Oxygen Atoms are in 5 US Tons of PETN?

PETN is the heaviest explosive of the three on this site, this is mainly because it has 12 Oxygen Atoms that make up more than 40% of PETN altogether. Oxygen is the heaviest atom in the entire formula at 15.999 molar mass. The total amount of atoms in PETN is 1.5842897403 * 1031. This explosive also has the most amount of atoms in it.

The chemical formula for this compound is [ C5H8N4O12 ]

Once again, the equation is the same on question 3 and the only things changed are the values of the atom being measured, molar mass of the compound and the amount of the atom in the compound. This equation measures the amount of Oxygen atoms in 5 US Tons of PETN. After running through this equation again, PETN has 1.036841888801374088 * 1029 Oxygen Atoms in it.

The calculations for getting the amount of Nitrogen atoms is as follows:

5 US Tons of PETN * (
907185gTNT / 1 US Ton of PETN
) * (
1 mol PETN / 316.137gPETN
) * (
12 mol O / 1 mol PETN
) * (
6.022*1023 O atoms / 1 mol O
) = 1.036841888801374088 * 1029 Oxygen Atoms
