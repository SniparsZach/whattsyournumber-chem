Question 2 - Ammonium Nitrate Fuel Oil (ANFO)

How Many Hydrogen Atoms are in 5 US Tons of ANFO?

Continuing off of some of the information Question 1 had, we already know how many grams are in 1 US Ton. Along with this information we knew from the previous question, we have a new explosive which means a new chemical compound. ANFO is 94.3% Ammonium Nitrate and 5.7% Fuel Oil and in this case I am saying Diesel Fuel instead of Fuel Oil.

The chemical formula for this explosive is, [ NH4NO3 + C12H23 ]. Ammonium Nitrate is [ NH4NO3 ] and Fuel Oil / Disel Fuel is [ C12H23 ].

The equation for this research question along with Question 1 and Question 3 are all the same except they have different values. The method of determining the equation remains the same except that the molar mass values, the atom being counted and the amount of that atom changes.

The molar mass of ANFO is the lightest of the other 2 explosives being investigated at 85.0176814 grams. Another thing to note is that the amount of atoms per mole of ANFO is 2.64968 * 1025 atoms

By replacing these values and going through the equation process again, there are 8.6748751822455348 * 1029 Hydrogen atoms in 5 US Tons of ANFO. Although ANFO is the lightest explosive of the three, it has more atoms in it that any of the other

The calculations for getting the amount of Nitrogen atoms is as follows:

5 US Tons of ANFO * (
907185gTNT / 1 US Ton of ANFO
) * (
1 mol ANFO / 85.01726814gANFO
) * (
27 mol H / 1 mol ANFO
) * (
6.022*1023 H atoms / 1 mol H
) = 8.6748751822455348 * 1029 Hydrogen Atoms
