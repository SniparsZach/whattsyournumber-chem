Question 1 - Trinitrotoluene (TNT)

How Many Nitrogen Atoms are in 5 US Tons of TNT?

The first thing I looked for about TNT was the chemical formula that makes up TNT. After that I had to determine the weight I was going to be measuring about this explosive. Seeing that the heavier the explosive compound, the bigger the blast, I decided on 5 US Tons. After I decided on the weight of the explosive I was going to be measuring, I had to convert from US Tons to grams.

According to Google, 5 US Tons is equivalent to 4,535,925 grams or 907,185 grams per US Ton.

After finding out how many grams are in 5 US Tons, I then needed to find the Chemical Formula for TNT and the molar mass. The Chemical Formula for TNT is [ C7H5N3O6 ] with the molar mass being 227.13 grams and the amount of molecules in 1 mole of TnT being 1.26462 * 1025.

Then to create an equation that would get us to figure out how many Nitrogen Atoms are in 5 US Tons of TnT. Since we need to find out how much Nitrogen is in 5 US Tons of TnT , then our equation should equal how many Nitrogen atoms are in 5 US Tons of TnT. We also have our beginning measurement which is 5 US Tons because we are trying to find how many Nitrogen atoms are in 5 US Tons . Therefor, our starting measurement is 5 US Tons and our ending measurement is going to be the total amount of Nitrogen Atoms in 5 US Tons of TnT. To fill in the difference, our first step from 5 US Tons is finding how many grams are in 1 US Ton, which is 907,185 grams. After that we grab the molar mass of TNT and compare it to how many moles it is equivalent to. After that, we multiply by how many moles of Nitrogen are in 1 mole of TNT. The second to last step of this step is get the amount of atoms in 1 US Ton, since in the beginning of the equation when we started to compare it was 1 US Ton to Moles of TNT. Our final step is to multiply the number by 5 to go from 1 US Ton to 5 US Tons.

The final number from this equation is 3.6078906815480122 * 1028 Nitrogen Atoms or in other words, more than 36 Octillion Nitrogen atoms.

The calculations for getting the amount of Nitrogen atoms is as follows:

5 US Tons of TnT * (
907185gTNT / 1 US Ton of TNT
) * (
1 mol TNT / 227.13gTNT
) * (
3 mol N / 1 mol TNT
) * (
6.022*1023 N atoms / 1 mol N
) = 3.6078906815480122 * 1028 Nitrogen Atoms
